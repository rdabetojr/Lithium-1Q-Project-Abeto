About

The Philippines education system still faces significant challenges, despite the ongoing reforms these past years. Some students reportedly have a high rate of illiteracy and poor fundamental skills. Particularly in rural areas, where there is a lack of basic utilities such as electricity and potable water, and proper instructional materials are in short supply. Furthermore, students perform poorly in international assessments like the PISA and TIMSS, which indicate the problems of education in our country. The following are the major difficulties encountered by the country's educational system.

Insufficient Funding

Insufficient funding in the Philippines' education results in a poor quality of infrastructure, overcrowded classrooms, and a lack of learning materials. These factors affect a student's academic performance, limiting their opportunities for economic advancements and social mobility.

Outdated Curricula

The outdated education curricula in the country affect graduates, who lack essential skills and knowledge for the modern workforce. That could lead to unemployment and hamper economic progress.
